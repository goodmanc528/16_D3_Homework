Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

Observation 1 - Poverty

The plotted information tells us that there is a positive linear correllation between the Poverty rate of a state and the three values on the X-Axis: Lacking Healthcare, Obesity Rate, Smoker Rate. This fairly alarming as those who seem at greater risk for health problems (overweight and/or smokers) are not receiving the proper healthcare and very likely do not have the means to pursure it themselves.

Observation 2 - Income

Look at the Median income we can see that there is a very loose negative linear correlation, at best, between Income and having sufficient healthcare. At the reported income levels over 80% of the residents in 49 (counting Washington DC) of states/districts have sufficient healthcare. Looking at obesity and smoker rate we see a stronger negative correltation between Income and Obesity and Smoker Rate. As a state's average income decreases they may see and increase in the number of smokers as well as an increase the number of obese residents.
